Cold War Timeline

Feburary 1945 - The Yalta Conference

The Yalta Conference was a conference tahing place during Febuary of 1945. The conference was in a Russian resort town, and Franklin D. Roosevelt, Winston Churchill, and Joseph Stalin met to make decisions about the war. The three agreed on splitting Germany into four zones. The USSR obtained East Germany, and France, the United States, and Britain would get West Germany. The United States would obtain an agreement from the USSR to assist in the fight against Japan, and defined policy for the regions liberated during World War II. The conference was important because it was when the USSR officially had control over Eastern Europe, and was recognized by the United States and Great Britain. This was considered to be the beginning of the Cold War in Europe.
