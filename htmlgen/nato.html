NATO was an alliance created in 1949 in order to combat the Soviets. USSR as a collective. The NATO Treaty was created in order to support European countries with protection from the Soviet Union and a potential German uprising. Eventually, the final version of the North Atlantic Treaty was signed in 1949 by 12 nations. The nations would support each other in the case of an attack against any member. This clause was especially important due to the United State's ownership of nuclear weapons, alongside NATO's collective adoption of "massive retaliation" principles. The combination of these two factors was meant to deter the Soviet Union from attacking.

In 1955, the members of NATO agreed to add the Federal Republic of Germany, also known as "West Germany," to the treaty. This action led to the Soviet Union's creation of a similar alliance, known as the Warsaw Pact.

U. S. Department of State, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

The Warsaw Pact was established in May of 1995 in retaliation against NATO. It consisted of the USSR, and some surrounding countries. These nations included Albania, Bulgaria, Hungary, East Germany, Poland, Romania, and Czechoslovakia. The treaty had similar effects to the North Atlantic Treaty, with each member coming to the defense of the others in the case of an attack.

The Warsaw Pact is sometimes referred to as the "eastern bloc", while NATO is the "western bloc."
