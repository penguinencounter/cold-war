Vietnam

The Vietnam War was one of many proxy wars that were a part of the Cold War. In this case, the war was between North Vietnam and South Vietnam. North Vietnam's goal was to unite both North and South Vietnam under North Vietnam's leader, Ho Chi Minh. The United States' goal was to prevent this from happening. The Soviet Union supported North Vietnam.

{{ sbs_img( "https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/99/Vietnam_war_1957_to_1960_map_english.svg", "Don-kun, NordNordWest, CC BY-SA 3.0 , via Wikimedia Commons", "422/599" ) }}

Notably, the Vietnam War was the first war to be televised in the United States. By 1966, over 500 thousand troops were deployed by the United States to Vietnam. The coverage of the war, alongside daily reports of military casualties, lead to criticism of the war effort from civilians at home. Due to the increased access to war media, people were exposed to the gruesome wartime conditions that the soldiers were in. However, the reporting also covered less laudable things. For example, in November of 1969, the newspapers covering the war reported that U.S. troops had massacred men, women, and children in a remote village. These factors lead to protests over the U.S.'s deployment into Vietnam, and whether the effort was worth it is still disputed today.

{{ sbs_img( "https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1a/Vietnam_War_on_television.jpg", "Warren K. Leffler, U.S. News & World Report Magazine, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons" ) }}

A key part of the war was the Tet Offensive. This attack occured on Tet, a traditional Vietnamese holiday, which celebrates the beginning of a new lunar year. This day is generally considered a ceasefire, but some North Vietnam leaders decided that an extreme offensive could be effective. The theory was that the combination of an unexpected attack with the already taxed South Vietnam citizens would result in an uprising of the South.

While the initial attack was mildly successful, even reaching the U.S. embassy in Saigon, the counterattack pushed the army back successfully. The result was the North Vietnamese suffering around 45 thousand casualties, while the South Vietnamese and United States only lost a few thousand.
