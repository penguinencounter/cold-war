Korean War

{%endcall%}

Similarly to Vietnam, after World War II, Korea had been split into two parts: North Korea and South Korea. The war began when the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (North Korea, abbreviated DPRK) attacked the Republic of Korea (South Korea, abbreviated ROK). The DPRK was trained and supported with weapons by the Soviet Union, as well as the People's Republic of China. The invasion (and the Korean War) began on June 25th, 1950, and by the 28th, the DPRK forces had captured Seoul, the South Korean capital.

In response to the attack, the United States administration took the issue of the ongoing war to the United Nations. The United States called for the DPRK forces to leave the area below the 38th parallel, where the line between North and South Korea was drawn after World War II. They also called for additional assistance to repel the attack. At this point, the United States already was providing support to the ROK forces by way of arms and air / water support.

Once the United States brought its military, alongside United Nations allies, they would push DPRK forces northward after recovering Seoul. This push would continue until the 19th of October. On that date, the Chinese People's Volunteers would begin to support the DPRK effort. Due to the increased volume of forces from the CPV, and the fact that the UN forces, in capturing a large amount of territory, had overextended itself, the CPV and DPRK successfully pushed the UN forces back to the 38th parallel.

The war ended on July 27, 1953, after both sides signed an armistice.
